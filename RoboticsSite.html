What We Do

Where Your Support Goes

Your sponsorship fuels every aspect of our program — from engineering and fabrication to community impact.

⚙️

Mechanical Engineering

Students design and fabricate the robot's structure, mechanisms, and drivetrain using industry-standard tools, CNC machining, and hands-on building.

💻

Software & Autonomous

Teams write code in Java and Python for autonomous routines, driver controls, computer vision, and sensor integration — real-world programming experience.

🔌

Electrical Systems

Wiring, power distribution, motor controllers, and sensor integration. Students learn circuit design and troubleshooting on complex robotic systems.

🎨

3D Design & CAD

Students create detailed 3D models and technical drawings in SolidWorks and Onshape, turning concepts into manufacturing-ready plans.

🏭

Fabrication & Prototyping

From 3D printing to CNC routing and metal work, students gain hands-on manufacturing skills building functional robot components.

🧪

Testing & Strategy

Rigorous testing cycles, match strategy analysis, and data-driven scouting teach students the scientific method and critical thinking.

📢

Business & Marketing

Students manage budgets, write grant proposals, build brand identity, and run social media — real business skills for real-world applications.

🎬

Media & Documentation

Photography, videography, and graphic design bring the season to life. Students produce content for social media, sponsor reports, and events.

🤝

Community Outreach

STEM workshops, school demos, and mentoring younger teams. Our students give back and inspire the next generation of innovators.

About Us

More Than Just Robots

Our robotics program at Santa Margarita Catholic High School in Rancho Santa Margarita, California is about more than building machines — it's about building skills, confidence, and community. With two competitive FRC teams under one roof, we offer students a place to discover engineering, programming, design, and leadership.

Whether you're into CAD, coding, machining, or marketing — there's a role for you. Our teams work side by side, sharing knowledge and resources while competing independently on the field.

Achievements

Our Track Record

From regional wins to community impact — here's what we've accomplished.

2026 Season

5805 — District Event Finalist

SMbly Required advanced to the playoff finals at the CA District Hueneme Port Event, losing a nail-biter championship match by a single point (169–170). Currently ranked #47 in the California District.

2025 Season

5805 — Regional Winners & Beach Blitz Champions

SMbly Required had a breakout year — winning both the Ventura County Regional and the Beach Blitz off-season event, and reaching the finals of the SoCal Showdown.

2022 Season

3020 — Orange County Regional Finalist

SMbld made a strong post-COVID return, advancing to the finals at the Orange County Regional.

2019 Season

5805 — Regional Finalist & World Championship

SMbly Required earned a Regional Finalist award and Wildcard at the Orange County Regional, punching their ticket to the FIRST Championship on the Turing Division.

2018 Season

5805 — Orange County Regional Winners

In just their third season, SMbly Required won the Orange County Regional and competed at the FIRST Championship on the Carver Division.

2016 Season

5805 — Rookie All Star & Highest Rookie Seed

SMbly Required burst onto the scene in their debut year, earning both the Rookie All Star Award and the Highest Rookie Seed at the San Diego Regional, then competing at the FIRST Championship.

2009 Season

3020 — Rookie All Star Award

SMbld launched the robotics program at Santa Margarita Catholic High School, earning the prestigious Rookie All Star Award at the Los Angeles Regional in their inaugural season.

Our Teams

Two Teams, One Mission

Each team designs, builds, and competes independently — but we share a shop, mentors, and a drive to win.

SMbld
